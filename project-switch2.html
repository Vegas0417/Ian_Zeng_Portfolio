Overview

The core question was simple: how does the interface stay consistent when the same device moves between contexts (docked play at a distance, handheld play up close, and quick sessions on the go)? The journal focuses on clarity of navigation, feedback states, and what the UI communicates about hardware constraints like battery, portability, and play time.

Notes I focused on

Docked vs. handheld clarity

In docked mode, text size, spacing, and contrast need to hold up at a distance. In handheld mode, density can increase, but tap targets and feedback timing still matter.

Signifiers and predictable states

Small cues such as icons, badges, and consistent state changes help people understand what is selectable, what is loading, and what just happened.

Constraints show up in the UI

Portability and battery shape how people play. When the interface communicates power, performance, and session limits, it reduces surprise and helps planning.

Takeaways

  • Keep a stable navigation model across modes so people do not relearn where key actions live.
  • Design feedback states that work for both short interactions (quick checks) and longer tasks (downloads, updates).
  • Make constraints legible: battery and performance are part of the user experience, not just hardware specs.
