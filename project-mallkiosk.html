Prototype

Mall Directory Kiosk Prototype

A kiosk UI concept focused on fast store discovery and clear next steps. Built as an interactive prototype to explore search, category browsing, and route guidance.

Platform
System UI (kiosk)
Work
Interface design, interaction flows, prototyping
Tools
Figma

Problem

Mall kiosks often feel slow and confusing when people are in a hurry. Users need to find a store quickly, confirm they picked the right one, and get a clear path without backtracking.

Goal

Make the find → route flow predictable: clear entry points, readable results, and a direction view that reduces decision fatigue.

Reasoning

Users and core tasks

  • Search a store by name
  • Browse by category when the name is unknown
  • Confirm the right destination with helpful details
  • Get a clear route and next step

Constraints

  • Public setting: quick decisions, minimal typing
  • Glare and distance: high readability
  • Short sessions: simple navigation and recovery

Design focus

  • Strong information hierarchy and large tap targets
  • Clear state changes and feedback
  • Consistent back paths and breadcrumbs

Key interaction decisions

Two entry modes: Search + Browse

Search supports people who know the destination. Browse supports people who only know a category. Both routes lead to the same store detail view.

Store detail as confirmation step

Before routing, show the store name, category, and a small location hint. This reduces mis-navigation and makes the next action feel safer.

Route view keeps one clear next action

The route screen prioritizes the first step and a simple overview. No extra options that compete with the primary goal.

Outcome

A clean prototype that communicates a predictable flow and makes the kiosk feel calmer: fewer decisions per screen, clearer recovery, and faster store confirmation.

What I learned

Kiosk UX is about speed and confidence. When the hierarchy is strong and the back path is obvious, people move faster without feeling rushed.
